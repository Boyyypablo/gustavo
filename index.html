Highlights

As a junior I'm still at the beginning of my career, but even so I value a lot all the experience I had until now. In this topic, you can check out with a little more detail some important moments of my career.

1. Co-author in a paper

In 2022 I was graced with an academic research scholarship to develop a project about the microstructural setting of a pluton intruded between two shear zones, in the Borborema Province (NE Brazil). Parallel to this project, my mentor was developing a paper about the magnetic fabric of this same pluton, which I was invited to contribute. We spent two weeks doing field activities in the region for structural mapping and sample collection for thin sections, which resulted in a thorough analysis of the deformation fabric in the pluton. When finished, the paper was published by the Journal of Structural Geology: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jsg.2022.104738

2. Serbian Internship

In the summer of 2023 I was part of the IAESTE exchange program. I was approved to be an intern for two months at RB Kolubara, a lignite coal mine in Serbia responsible for approximately 70% of the energy in the country. At the mine I did a lot of research about the regional geology and genesis of the deposit, as well as being part of a series of field activities where I accompanied a couple of core-drilling and logging campaigns, also did tests for hydrogeological wells, and mapping for the advance of the mining front, and much more.

3. Final Thesis

The final thesis of the University of Brasília is a bit different from other universities in Brazil. Every year the whole class of graduates gets selected a region around Brasília to realize a complete and detailed mapping on a 1:50000 scale. Our class completed 21 days of field work in the Correntina-Jaborandi region in Bahia (NE Brazil), located in the western zone of the São Francisco Craton. We conducted an extensive petrographic and structural characterization, as well as interpretations of the metamorphic and tectonic settings and their potential mineralization systems. In addition, we even incorporated a hydrogeological assessment and mapping of the ground water system for the region. This type of thesis extracts the most of the knowledge acquired during graduation, in every area of geology!

4. Geophysical Survey

After graduating, I worked as a field assistant in two geophysical surveys. The first one was an airborne magnetometry (Mag Drone) survey in the Center-West region of Brazil, and the second was a 40 days survey for IP and MT data acquisition. Both these experiences were extremely enriching since I had to be extremely attentive to detail to handle geophysical equipment, and I was working with a very diverse crew, having to relay and translate info during both campaigns.
